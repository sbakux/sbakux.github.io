My approach to leadership and design.

 

Design Leadership means empowering someone get further than they could on their own. This is done through trust, accountability and collaborative facilitation. With such conditions, designers can help teams to engage in iterative and insight-driven processes to help businesses and organizations by transforming empathic user insights into strong experiences that are technologically feasible and strategically viable.

01 — Design Leadership

Leading teams & driving maturity

Design leadership requires a structured approach to analyzing and building capabilities for design teams that aligns with the business and organisational needs

  • Organisation:

    Drive a design-led culture to empower design teams by establishing a shared language around human-centric approaches across the organisation. Align with stakeholders and other business units to promote the capabilities of the design team and support external and internal business problems.

  • Operations:

    Enable creative problem-solving and and ensure a healthy team by putting designers in a position to focus on their own growth and craft. This comes with defining processes, designing environments and setting up everything from processes to delivery to tools.

  • Experience:

    Support innovative delivery with a good velocity to bring great product experiences. Align with multidisciplinary agile teams and give design a clear seat at the table. Help empower decision-making and provide leadership when necessary.

  • Team:

    Manage and build the design team through a collaborative partnership, where trust and growth is of most importance. Foster an inspiring team culture and accountability through intervision and shared responsibility built on growth scales and good hiring. Lastly, ensure smart onboarding and clear and transparent career paths.

02 — Strategic Design

Value-creation and organizational goals

I strongly advocate using design approaches to uncover competitive advantages for businesses and organizations, and working in the intersection between design, engineering and business development. Trough our methods of envisioning the future, sketching, and collaboration, design is uniquely positioned to help drive businesses forward.

I approach design strategically by:

  • Leading problem definition and project scoping
  • Uncovering organizational goals and success criteria
  • Formulating visions and roadmaps
  • Presenting solution ideas stakeholders and management
  • Tying design rationales back to business objectives

03 — UX design

Empathy-driven design

UX design is about creating solutions that are both meaningful for users as well as good for business. Good design is crafting purposeful digital interactions, and by understanding the broader ecosystem, the product exists in.

To me, UX design is

  • Spotting the right opportunity to frame an experience.
  • Building meaningful and useful experiences that are condictive to people's everday rhythm.
  • Fluctuating between attention to interaction details and ecosystem.
  • Navigating user behavior, organisational needs, and product development.
  • Being intentional about design decisions based on insights.
  • Planning and facilitating collaborative, creative processes.
  • Part of the entire process from vision, through ideation to implementation.
