About

I hold a degree in Digital Design from the IT University of Copenhagen, where I have specialized in interaction design and did a thesis concerning user experience in smart homes of the future. Besides shorter UX projects, I have later on been a UX designer & researcher at Adaptive Environments and furthermore co-lecturer of the courses ‘Experimental Interaction’, ‘Digital media’ and ‘Creative Digital Practice’ at the ITU.

This is supplemented with a background in philosophy from the University of Copenhagen, where I focused on human experience and aesthetics alongside minors in innovation and HCI.

This has given me the ability to rapidly gain critical perspectives on projects, and I believe a holistic and agile approach to technology can pave the way for new innovative products and environments.

My experience resides in handling relations to partners, just as well as handling the human-centered design process and the intangibles of UX research. I wish to challenge the boundaries of technology in order to design for innovative, meaningful and pleasurable user-experiences with interactive products and applications. Therefore my work ranges from commercial concepts to more explorative investigations in digital technology.

Recently I’ve been leading the workshops and UX research of a project focused on user-centric design of intelligent lighting within SaaS and IOT.
